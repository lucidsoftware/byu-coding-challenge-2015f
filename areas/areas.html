Description

Since you don't have any friends, you get to spend Saturday morning solving a geometry problem.
A concave quadrilateral can be divided into four non-overlapping triangles by connecting opposite vertices, like so:
Given the four vertices of the quadrilateral, you are to find the area of the largest of the four triangles created in this way.
You may find the "shoelace formula" helpful. It describes the area of a degree-n polygon with vertices x 1 y 1 x 2 y 2 ... x n y n : A = 1 2 | ( ∑ i = 1 n - 1 x i y i + 1 ) + x n y 1 - ( ∑ i = 1 n - 1 x i + 1 y i ) - x 1 y n | which, when n=3, is A = 1 2 | x 1 y 2 + x 2 y 3 + x 3 y 1 - x 2 y 1 - x 3 y 2 - x 1 y 3 |

Input

The input is the four vertices, in order. Each vertex is on a line, and each line has the x- and y-coordinates separated by a space. All coordinates are integers from -500 to 500 inclusive.
The guaranteed is guarantted concave; i.e. the angle at each vertex is less than 180 degrees.

Output

Output a single number: the area of the largest triangle. This answer must be accurate to within 0.001.
