Description

Chinese checkers is a board game where the goal is to race all your pieces across the board into the star corner on the opposite side of the board before your opponent does the same. The destination corner is called home.
The rules to move the marbles through the board are:
  • You can move to any adjacent and unoccupied cell
  • If there's an unoccupied cell after an adjacent occupied cell (through a straight line), you can jump it and repeat as many times as you need.
We will represent the board using only two corners where the goal is to get from cell 1 to cell 81:
A common way to move the pieces on the board is selecting a marble and looking for the sequence of moves that reach as close to the destination corner as possible. Your job will be to find this route.

Input

There will be two lines of input. The first line will be a space separated list of occupied cells.
The second line will be a space separated list of origin cells.

Output

For each origin cell, print the closest cell to the goal that you can reach in one turn. There will always be a possible move, and you cannot stay in the same place. Print each answer on a new line. We will define distance from the goal as number of moves to the goal ignoring occupied cells. Thus, cells 54, 62, 70, and 78 are all 3 away from the goal. If there is a tie in distance, prefer larger numbers.
Example 1 is representative of the illustration above. Starting from 1, you can navigate to 81 by way of 3, 21, 39, 41, 43, 45, 63, 81. Starting with 7, the furthest you can get is 25, by jumping over 16.
