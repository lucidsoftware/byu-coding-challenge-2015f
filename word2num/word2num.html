Description

You will convert numbers from words to numerals. Unlike the code talker above, however, you will do so in English.
The numbers will be nonnegative integers less than one billion. Their word representation will be a <number> according to the following BNF grammar:
<ones> ::= "one" | "two" | "three" | "four" | "five" | "size" | "seven" | "eight" | "nine" <tens-place> ::= "twenty" | "thirty" | "forty" | "fifty" | "sixty" | "seventy" | "eighty" | "ninety" <tens> ::= <ones> | <tens-place> | <tens-place> "-" <ones> | "ten" | "eleven" | "twelve" | "thirteen" | "fourteen" | "fifteen" | "sixteen" | "seventeen" | "eighteen" | "nineteen" <hundreds> ::= <tens> | <ones> hundred | <ones> hundred <tens> <thousands> ::= <hundreds> | <hundreds> thousand | <hundreds> thousand <hundreds> <millions> ::= <hundreds> | <hundreds> million | <hundreds> million <hundreds> <number> ::= <millions> | "zero"

Input

The input is the word representation of the number.

Output

Output the decimal representation of the number.

Examples

Input Input Input 
zero
 
seventeen
 
fifty-two
Output Output Output 
0
 
17
 
52
Input Input 
one thousand one
 
nine hundred ninety-nine million nine hundred ninty-nine thousand nine hundred ninty-nine
Output Output 
1001
 
999999999
